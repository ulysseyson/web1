Musical style

Queen drew artistic influence from British rock acts of the 1960s and early 1970s, such as the Beatles, the Kinks, Cream, Led Zeppelin, Pink Floyd, the Who, Black Sabbath, Slade, Deep Purple, David Bowie, Genesis and Yes,[243] in addition to American guitarist Jimi Hendrix,[244] with Mercury also inspired by the gospel singer Aretha Franklin.[245] May referred to the Beatles as being "our bible in the way they used the studio and they painted pictures and this wonderful instinctive use of harmonies."[246] At their outset in the early 1970s, Queen's music has been characterised as "Led Zeppelin meets Yes" due to its combination of "acoustic/electric guitar extremes and fantasy-inspired multi-part song epics".[247] In his book, Essential Hard Rock and Heavy Metal, Eddie Trunk described Queen as "a hard rock band at the core but one with a high level of majesty and theatricality that delivered a little something for everyone", as well as observing that the band "sounded British".[248] Queen composed music that drew inspiration from many different genres of music, often with a tongue-in-cheek attitude.[249] The music styles and genres they have been associated with include progressive rock,[250][251] symphonic rock,[251][252] art rock,[19][253] glam rock,[250] arena rock,[250] heavy metal,[250] pop rock,[250] psychedelic rock,[254] baroque pop,[255] and rockabilly.[255] Queen also wrote songs that were inspired by diverse musical styles which are not typically associated with rock groups, such as opera,[256] music hall,[256] folk music,[257] gospel,[258] ragtime,[259] and dance/disco.[260] Their 1980 single "Another One Bites the Dust" became a major hit single in the funk rock genre.[261] Several Queen songs were written with audience participation in mind, such as "We Will Rock You" and "We Are the Champions".[262][263] Similarly, "Radio Ga Ga" became a live favourite because it would have "crowds clapping like they were at a Nuremberg rally".[264] "We Will Rock You" Menu 0:00 A sample of "We Will Rock You". In 1963, the teenage Brian May and his father custom-built his signature guitar Red Special, which was purposely designed to feedback.[265][266] May has used Vox AC30 amplifiers almost exclusively since a meeting with his long-time hero Rory Gallagher at a gig in London during the late 1960s/early 1970s.[267] Sonic experimentation figured heavily in Queen's songs. A distinctive characteristic of Queen's music are the vocal harmonies which are usually composed of the voices of May, Mercury, and Taylor best heard on the studio albums A Night at the Opera and A Day at the Races. Some of the ground work for the development of this sound can be attributed to the producer Roy Thomas Baker and engineer Mike Stone.[268][269] Besides vocal harmonies, Queen were also known for multi-tracking voices to imitate the sound of a large choir through overdubs. For instance, according to Brian May, there are over 180 vocal overdubs in "Bohemian Rhapsody".[270] The band's vocal structures have been compared with the Beach Boys,[253][271] but May stated they were not "much of an influence".[246] David Mallet directed a number of their music videos, some of which use footage from classic films: "Under Pressure" incorporates 1920s silent films, Sergei Eisenstein's Battleship Potemkin and F. W. Murnau's Nosferatu, the 1984 video for "Radio Ga Ga" includes footage from Fritz Lang's Metropolis (1927), while the 1995 video "Heaven for Everyone" shows footage from Georges Méliès' A Trip to the Moon (1902) and The Impossible Voyage (1904).[272] The first part of Mallet's music video for "I Want to Break Free" spoofed the popular long-running British soap opera Coronation Street.[273] The music video for "Innuendo" combines stop motion animation with rotoscoping and band members appear as illustrations and images taken from earlier Queen music videos on a cinema screen in the same manner as in the British film Nineteen Eighty-Four.[274] Legacy In 2002, Queen's "Bohemian Rhapsody" was voted "the UK's favourite hit of all time" in a poll conducted by the Guinness World Records British Hit Singles Book.[275] In 2004, the song was inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame.[276] Many scholars consider the "Bohemian Rhapsody" music video ground-breaking, crediting it with popularising the medium.[50][51] Rock historian Paul Fowles stated that the song is "widely credited as the first global hit single for which an accompanying video was central to the marketing strategy".[277] It has been hailed as launching the MTV age.[278] In December 2018, "Bohemian Rhapsody" became the most-streamed song from the 20th century, and the most-streamed classic rock song of all time.[279] The number of downloads of the song and original video exceeded 1.6 billion across global on-demand streaming services.[280] Acclaimed for their stadium rock, in 2005 an industry poll ranked Queen's performance at Live Aid in 1985 as the best live act in history.[262][281] In 2007, they were also voted the greatest British band in history by BBC Radio 2 listeners.[282] If you have to go back and watch one performance it has to be Queen. That was not just a career-defining moment ... rock 'n' roll might have peaked right there. Freddie Mercury on stage at Live Aid is the summit of rock 'n' roll. For a band some people said was washed up, that was just the most stunning, breathtaking, flawless, powerful, jubilant performance. It was everything you want in rock 'n' roll. —1980s MTV VJ Martha Quinn speaking in July 2015 on the 30th anniversary of Live Aid.[283] As of 2005, according to the Guinness Book of World Records, Queen albums have spent a total of 1,322 weeks (twenty-six years) on the UK Album Charts, more time than any other act.[91] Also in 2005, with the release of their live album with Paul Rodgers, Queen moved into third place on the list of acts with the most aggregate time spent on the British record charts.[284] In 2006, the Greatest Hits album was the all-time best-selling album in UK Chart history, with sales of 5,407,587 copies, over 604,295 more copies than its nearest competitor, the Beatles' Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band.[92] Their Greatest Hits II album is the tenth best seller, with sales of 3,746,404 copies.[151][285] The band have released a total of eighteen number one albums, eighteen number one singles, and ten number one DVDs worldwide, making them one of the world's best-selling music artists. Queen have sold over 170 million records, with some estimates in excess of 300 million records worldwide,[262][286][287][288][289] including 34.5 million albums in the US as of 2004.[290] Inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2001, the band is the only group in which every member has composed more than one chart-topping single, and all four members were inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame in 2003.[291][292] In 2009, "We Will Rock You" and "We Are the Champions" were inducted into the Grammy Hall of Fame,[293][294] and the latter was voted the world's favourite song in a 2005 Sony Ericsson global music poll.[295] The band received Ivor Novello Awards for Outstanding Contribution to British Music, in 1987, and Outstanding Song Collection, in 2005, from the British Academy of Songwriters, Composers, and Authors.[296][297] In 2018, they were presented the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award.[298] Queen are one of the most bootlegged bands ever, according to Nick Weymouth, who manages the band's official website.[299] A 2001 survey discovered the existence of 12,225 websites dedicated to Queen bootlegs, the highest number for any band.[300] Bootleg recordings have contributed to the band's popularity in certain countries where Western music is censored, such as Iran.[301] In a project called Queen: The Top 100 Bootlegs, many of these have been made officially available to download for a nominal fee from Queen's website, with profits going to the Mercury Phoenix Trust.[299] Rolling Stone ranked Queen at number 52 on its list of the "100 Greatest Artists of All Time",[302] while ranking Mercury the 18th greatest singer,[150] and May the twenty-sixth greatest guitarist.[303] Rolling Stone readers voted Mercury the second greatest frontman.[304] Queen were named 13th on VH1's 100 Greatest Artists of Hard Rock list,[305] and in 2010 were ranked 17th on VH1's 100 Greatest Artists of All Time list.[306] In 2012, Gigwise readers named Queen the best band of the past 60 years.[307] Eddie Trunk argued that Queen's importance, in common with Thin Lizzy and Deep Purple, was "not as fully recognized" in the United States because elsewhere they "often played to much bigger crowds in stadium venues".[248] Queen had stopped touring the US in 1982 as their success there had started to wane, but they remained a touring "juggernaut", filling stadiums and arenas internationally in the 1980s until their final tour (with Mercury) in 1986.[255] Influence At 10 years old, Thom Yorke of Radiohead made a homemade guitar in an attempt to emulate Brian May. Queen have been credited with making a significant contribution to genres such as hard rock and heavy metal.[248][308] The band have been cited as an influence by many other musicians. Moreover, like their music, the bands and artists that have claimed to be influenced by Queen or have expressed admiration for them are diverse, spanning different generations, countries, and genres, including heavy metal: Judas Priest,[309] Iron Maiden,[310] Metallica,[311] Dream Theater,[312] Trivium,[313] Megadeth,[314] Anthrax,[315] Faith No More,[316] Melvins,[317] Slipknot[318] and Rage Against the Machine;[319] hard rock: Guns N' Roses,[320] Def Leppard,[321] Van Halen,[322] Mötley Crüe,[323] Steve Vai,[324] the Cult,[325] the Darkness,[326] Kid Rock[327] and Foo Fighters;[328] alternative rock: Nirvana,[329] Radiohead,[330] Trent Reznor,[331] Muse,[332] Red Hot Chili Peppers,[333] Jane's Addiction,[334] the Flaming Lips,[335] and The Smashing Pumpkins;[336] shock rock: Marilyn Manson;[337] pop rock: Meat Loaf,[338] The Killers,[339] My Chemical Romance,[340] Fall Out Boy[341] and Panic! at the Disco;[342] and pop: Michael Jackson,[343] George Michael,[344] Robbie Williams,[345] Adele,[346] Lady Gaga,[347] Katy Perry,[348] Kesha,[349] Grimes,[350] and Psy.[351] In the early 1970s, Queen helped spur the heavy metal genre's evolution by discarding much of its blues influence.[352][353] Queen's 1974 song "Stone Cold Crazy" has been cited as a precursor of speed metal.[354] Metallica recorded a cover version of "Stone Cold Crazy", which first appeared on the Rubáiyát: Elektra's 40th Anniversary album in 1990, and won their second Grammy Award for Best Metal Performance in 1991.[355] Thom Yorke of Radiohead received his first guitar at 7 years old, encouraged after seeing Brian May in a broadcast of a Queen concert.[330] At 10 years old, Yorke made his own homemade guitar, trying to imitate what May had done with his Red Special, but he was not satisfied with the result.[356] Subsequently, Queen was one of the first influences in the music of Radiohead.[330]
